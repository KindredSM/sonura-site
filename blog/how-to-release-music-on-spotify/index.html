How to Release Music on Spotify: Complete Guide for Independent Artists 2025

October 20, 2025 • 15 min read

Want to release your music on Spotify? This comprehensive guide covers everything from choosing a distributor to getting playlisted and maximizing your streams as an independent artist in 2025.

Why Release on Spotify?

spotify is the world's largest music streaming platform with over 600 million users. for independent artists, spotify offers:

  • global reach: your music accessible worldwide instantly
  • discoverability: algorithm-driven playlists expose you to new fans
  • revenue: streaming royalties paid directly to you
  • data & analytics: detailed insights about your listeners
  • credibility: being on spotify legitimizes you as an artist

in 2025, spotify pays approximately $0.003-0.005 per stream. while it takes many streams to make significant money, spotify is crucial for building a fanbase and music career.

Step 1: Prepare Your Music for Release

audio quality requirements

  • format: wav or flac (lossless)
  • sample rate: 44.1 khz minimum (48 khz preferred)
  • bit depth: 16-bit minimum (24-bit preferred)
  • loudness: -14 lufs integrated (spotify normalizes to this)
  • true peak: -1 db tp maximum (avoid clipping)

pro tip: don't over-compress or "brick wall" your masters. spotify's normalization will turn down loud tracks anyway. prioritize dynamic range and clarity.

metadata essentials

prepare this information before uploading:

  • track title: exact spelling and capitalization
  • artist name: exactly as you want it displayed
  • album/ep/single name: release title
  • genre and subgenre: accurate categorization
  • release date: when you want it live
  • copyright info: © year artist name
  • isrc codes: unique identifiers (distributor usually provides)

album artwork

requirements:

  • dimensions: 3000x3000 pixels (minimum), 4000x4000 recommended
  • format: jpg or png
  • aspect ratio: perfect square (1:1)
  • color space: rgb (not cmyk)
  • content: no urls, social media handles, or pricing

Step 3: Upload and Schedule Your Release

timing your release

minimum lead time: submit at least 2-3 weeks before your desired release date

optimal lead time: 4-6 weeks allows time for playlist pitching

best release days:

  • friday: industry standard, aligns with playlist updates
  • thursday: less competition than friday
  • avoid mondays (lowest engagement)

the upload process

regardless of distributor, the process is similar:

  1. create account with distributor
  2. upload audio files (wav/flac)
  3. upload artwork (3000x3000+ jpg)
  4. enter metadata (title, artist, genre, etc.)
  5. select platforms (spotify, apple music, etc.)
  6. set release date
  7. pay fees
  8. submit for review

pro tip: enable pre-save campaigns. this lets fans save your music before release, boosting first-day streams.

Step 4: Claim Your Spotify for Artists Profile

once your music is live (or submitted), claim your artist profile at artists.spotify.com:

why it's essential

  • customize your artist profile (bio, photos, social links)
  • access detailed analytics and listener data
  • pitch unreleased songs to spotify playlists
  • manage your artist pick (highlighted track)
  • connect with fans through canvas (looping video backgrounds)

optimizing your profile

  • artist photo: high-quality, recognizable, consistent with brand
  • bio: concise, compelling, updated regularly
  • social links: connect instagram, twitter, facebook
  • artist pick: feature your best or newest track
  • gallery: add live photos, behind-the-scenes content

Step 5: Pitch to Spotify Playlists

playlisting is the #1 way to grow on spotify. there are three types of playlists:

editorial playlists

curated by spotify's team. getting on these can give you hundreds of thousands of streams.

how to pitch:

  • use spotify for artists dashboard
  • pitch before release (at least 7 days)
  • write compelling pitch (genre, mood, story behind song)
  • be honest and specific
  • only pitch your best song from a release

algorithmic playlists

generated by spotify's algorithm based on listening habits. examples: discover weekly, release radar, radio.

how to get on them:

  • high completion rate (listeners play full song)
  • saves and playlist adds
  • consistent release schedule
  • strong first-week performance

user-generated playlists

created by regular users and independent curators. easiest to get on but smaller reach.

how to approach:

  • find playlists in your genre using spotify search
  • look for curator contact info in playlist description
  • use submission platforms: submithub, playlist push
  • be professional and personal in your pitch
  • follow up politely if no response after a week

Step 6: Promote Your Release

pre-release promotion (2-4 weeks before)

  • announce release date on social media
  • share artwork and snippets
  • set up pre-save campaign
  • email your mailing list
  • reach out to music blogs and influencers

release week strategy

  • release day: post everywhere, share spotify link, thank supporters
  • days 2-3: share lyrics, behind-the-scenes content
  • days 4-7: fan reactions, playlist adds, streaming milestones

long-term promotion

  • continue pitching to curators
  • create content around the song (tiktoks, reels, youtube videos)
  • submit to music blogs and online magazines
  • perform live (in-person or streaming)
  • collaborate with other artists for cross-promotion

Understanding Spotify Royalties

payment structure

spotify pays approximately $0.003 to $0.005 per stream in 2025. your exact rate depends on:

  • listener's country (premium vs free)
  • listener's subscription tier
  • total streams on spotify that month

realistic expectations

  • 1,000 streams = $3-5
  • 10,000 streams = $30-50
  • 100,000 streams = $300-500
  • 1 million streams = $3,000-5,000

remember: spotify is about building an audience, not immediate income. focus on growing your fanbase.

Conclusion: Your Spotify Journey

releasing music on spotify as an independent artist in 2025 is more accessible than ever. key takeaways:

  • choose the right distributor for your release frequency
  • prepare high-quality audio and metadata
  • claim your spotify for artists profile immediately
  • pitch to playlists (editorial and user-generated)
  • promote consistently before, during, and after release
  • focus on building audience, not just streams

the most important thing: release consistently. the algorithm favors active artists. aim for a new release every 4-8 weeks to maintain momentum.
